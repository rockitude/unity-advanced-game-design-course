Students who take the course in person are expected to maintain active participation during class meeting times. This course gives the student opportunities to work on whichever available topic interests them, so it is expected that you will choose and focus on a course-related topic during your time in class. Any time spent on pursuits not related to this course will be reflected in your daily participation grade.

Spelling, Grammar, Punctuation and Content

You are expected to use spelling, grammar and punctuation correctly in any assignment that involves text. You should always answer questions in your own words, and always use complete sentences. Points will be taken off for incorrect spelling, bad grammar, incorrect punctuation and incomplete sentences.

Etiquette

In an online or blended course, students will be expected to maintain a level of discourse similar to that of an in-person classroom. Correct spelling, grammar and punctuation should be used at all times. In person, students will be expected to abide by school rules and discussion expectations. On written assignments (especially within a game), correct spelling, grammar and punctuation are expected.

Your Data

Bring your backup drive to class every day. Keep backup copies of your work on it. It is also a good idea to keep a second backup in an online storage service such as Google Drive, Dropbox or Box.net. Unpredictable things happen with computer hard drives, and it is possible that your data could be lost. Having a backup protects you in case things go awry.

Plagiarism

Plagiarism is absolutely not tolerated. All assignments, including short worksheets, must be written in your own words. Copying and pasting from a website is considered plagiarism and will result in a grade of zero.

Email

Element

Acceptable Format/Content

Unacceptable Format/Content

Subject line
  • relevant to purpose
  • factual tone (unemotional)
  • slight detail (e.g. “prepping for Thoreau quiz”)
  • empty or irrelevant to purpose
  • emotional (e.g. “HELLLLLP!!!”)
  • vague detail (e.g. “HW”)

Introduction/Greeting
  • includes greeting (e.g. “Dear...” “Good afternoon..”)
  • includes pleasantries (e.g. “How are you?” “I hope you had a nice vacation/weekend”)
  • contains informal greeting (e.g. “hey” or “Yo”)
  • jumps right into question or demand

Body
  • brief and focused
  • contains enough background info/context to make sense
  • polite and respectful
  • contains requests, not demands
  • long-winded or unclear
  • offers no background or specific content to make request/question clear
  • emotional or panicked (e.g. “My parents are going to kill me!!!!!”)
  • demanding (e.g. “You have to let me retake that quiz!” “Give me...!” “Get me...!”)

Closing
  • acknowledges their time spent (e.g. “Thank you” or “I appreciate your help”)
  • respectful and friendly (“Have a great weekend!” or ““Sincerely”)
  • no closing
  • informal (e.g. “See ya later” or “C-Ya”)

Mechanics/Style
  • conventions of standard English followed (punctuation, capitalization, grammar, etc)
  • greeting, body, and closing are separate lines/paragraphs for visual ease
  • appropriate tone (semi-formal/friendly, respectful)
  • non-standard English (texting language, slang, informal style)
  • rude, demanding, or histrionic attitude
